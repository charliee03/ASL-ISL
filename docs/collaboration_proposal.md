### **The Project: AI-Powered ASL ➔ ISL Translation**
Our team has developed an end-to-end **Sign Language Recognition and Translation System** that:
1. **Captures** American Sign Language (ASL) gestures through a standard webcam.
2. **Translates** the sign glosses into Indian Sign Language (ISL) using advanced grammar restructuring and specialized Natural Language Processing (NLP).
3. **Generates** a smooth, real-time 2D avatar animation representing the translated ISL gestures on screen, helping deaf or hard-of-hearing individuals understand and communicate across regional boundaries.

This tool is designed to be highly extensible. We can deploy it as an interactive learning system in schools, clinics, or centers working with deaf, mute, or differently-abled children, providing them with a barrier-free communication bridge.

### **The Opportunity: IEEE EPICS Funding**
We are planning to apply for the prestigious **IEEE EPICS (Engineering Projects in Community Service)** grant. This grant provides funding of **US$1,000 to US$10,000** for student-led engineering projects that solve local community challenges under the "Access and Abilities" pillar.

**Key details of the funding:**
* **No Financial Cost to Parivartan:** All project materials, development hardware, and deployment costs are fully funded by the IEEE grant.
* **Community Partner Role:** IEEE EPICS requires us to partner with a registered non-profit organization. As our partner, Parivartan would help guide the local deployment of the software in your centers, collect user feedback from children and educators, and ensure the system's long-term community impact.
* **Funding Outcome:** The funding goes entirely toward building, refining, and installing this software for your organization’s permanent use.
